My Dolpin, you only guide me by surprise,
a captive as Racine, the man of craft,
drawn through his maze of iron composition by
the incomparable wandering voice of Phedre.
When I was troubled in mind, you made for my body
cought in its hangman's-knot of sinking lines,
the glassy bowing and scraping of my will...
I have sat and listened to too many
words of the collaborating muse,
and plotted perhaps to freely with my life,
not avoid injury to others,
not avoid injury to myself
to ask compassion... this book, half fiction,
an eelnet made by man for the eel fighting
my eyes have seen what my hand did.
